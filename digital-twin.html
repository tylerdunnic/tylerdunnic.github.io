Interactive Demo

Digital Twin Prototype

A live, browser-rendered Three.js simulation of a 12-station assembly line — fixtures travel station to station, one fails functional test and triggers a line stop, then clears on retest, all while KPIs update in real time.

This is illustrative, not real production data. The station layout, timing, and event data are generic/fictionalized — inspired by manufacturing internship experience with automated lines, but not a reproduction of any employer's actual process. Built to demonstrate real-time 3D web programming and applied data visualization.

Fixtures spawn every ~4 seconds and travel through 12 stations. Two fixtures (FX-004, FX-012) are pre-designated to fail functional test on the first pass, triggering a line stop until they clear retest — watch the KPI bar and event log react in real time.

How It Works

RFID tags are applied to each product at the head of the line, and sensors positioned at intervals down the line capture its progress, timestamping every station transition. Additional sensors detect when the line stops, identify which station triggered the stop — since each stop button is mapped to its station — and measure how long it stayed down. In a production deployment, this same event stream (RFID reads, station timestamps, stop/start events) could feed directly into a SQL database, giving engineering and quality teams a full, queryable history of line performance rather than a point-in-time snapshot.
